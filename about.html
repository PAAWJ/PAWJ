About PAWJ

Our mission, history, and the people behind the association.

Mission & Cause

PAWJ (Parent Association for Welfare & Justice) exists to empower parents and students by championing transparent, fair, and accountable practices in school education. Founded to address systemic gaps in rights awareness and to challenge exploitative fee policies, PAWJ believes every family deserves access to inclusive, affordable learning. We work together to ensure schools comply with laws and standards, support parents’ voices in decision-making, and protect children’s interests through lawful advocacy, community education, and collective action. Our mission is to build a strong, informed parent community that stands united for justice, quality education, and the well-being of every child.

History & Growth

Origin Story

The roots of PAWJ trace back to 2020, when a small group of parents banded together after repeated reports of opaque fee hikes and inconsistent compliance with school regulations. Motivated by a shared desire to protect children’s educational rights and prevent exploitative practices, this parents group began meeting informally, sharing resources, and documenting issues across multiple schools. Their early work focused on educating families about their legal rights, building a reliable network of concerned parents, and forming a united voice that could be heard by school management and authorities.

Growth Timeline

What began as a handful of concerned parents quickly evolved into an organised association. As the group amplified its voice through community outreach and public awareness campaigns, membership grew steadily—especially in response to high-profile fee disputes that drew local media attention. Throughout 2021 and beyond, the parents group organised coordinated letter-writing efforts, met with education officials, and supported legal actions where necessary. By 2023, the group had established a formal governing body, secured a registered memorandum, and became a recognized advocate for transparent, affordable, and accountable education policies.
