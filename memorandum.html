MEMORANDUM OF ASSOCIATION
(Under the Societies Registration Act, 1860, as applicable to the NCT of Delhi)
MEMORANDUM OF ASSOCIATION 
OF
PARENT RIGHTS EDUCATION ASSOCIATION

1. NAME OF THE SOCIETY
The name of the Society shall be “PARENT RIGHTS EDUCATION ASSOCIATION”
(hereinafter referred to as “the Society”).
The name does not contain any word implying patronage or approval of the Government and is not in violation of the Emblems and Names (Prevention of Improper Use) Act, 1950.

2. REGISTERED OFFICE
The registered office of the society shall be situated in the National Capital Territory of Delhi.
At present, it is located at:
A-19, Khazan Basti, Nangal Raya, New Delhi - 110046
The Governing Body may shift the Registered Office within the NCT of Delhi by passing a resolution and informing the Registrar of Societies, Delhi.

3. AREA OF OPERATION
The area of operation of the Society shall be Delhi and, in future, may extend to any part of India, as may be decided by the Governing Body in accordance with law after following the due procedure.

4. NATURE OF THE SOCIETY
The Society shall be:
• Non-profit
• Non-political
• Voluntary
• Charitable
• Secular
The Society shall function solely for public welfare purposes and shall not carry on any commercial or profit-making activity.

5. AIMS & OBJECTS OF THE SOCIETY
The Society is established with the following aims and objects:

A. OBJECTS RELATING TO EDUCATION AND PARENTS’ RIGHTS
a. To protect, promote, and safeguard the rights and interests of parents and students in matters relating to school education.
b. To ensure transparency, accountability, and lawful conduct by private, unaided, recognised schools.
c. To challenge illegal, arbitrary, excessive, and unapproved fee hikes and other exploitative practices affecting students and parents.
d. To ensure effective implementation of education laws, rules, regulations, government orders, circulars, and judicial directions.
e. To collectively represent parents and students before the Directorate of Education and other statutory or regulatory authorities.
f. To promote quality, inclusive, equitable, and affordable education for all sections of society.
g. To conduct awareness programmes, consultations, workshops, seminars, and lawful advocacy initiatives relating to parents’ and students’ rights in education.

B. LEGAL ADVOCACY & REPRESENTATION OBJECTS
a. To file, initiate, support, assist, or intervene in writ petitions, public interest litigations (PILs), appeals, complaints, and other legal proceedings before courts, tribunals, quasi-judicial bodies, and authorities in matters concerning education, public interest, and welfare of students and parents.
b. To submit representations, memorandums, objections, and suggestions before government departments, statutory bodies, commissions, and authorities.
c. To work for effective enforcement of laws, schemes, policies, and court directions relating to education and public welfare.

C. OBJECTS RELATING TO SOCIAL WELFARE
a. To work for the welfare and upliftment of disadvantaged, marginalised, and economically weaker sections, including children and parents.
b. To promote social justice, equality, dignity, and protection against exploitation in the field of education.

D. GENERAL & INCIDENTAL OBJECTS
a. To receive grants, donations, subscriptions, aid, and contributions from lawful sources.
b. To acquire, hold, manage, and dispose of movable and immovable property solely for the purposes of the Society.
c. To collaborate with government bodies, NGOs, trusts, institutions, and other organisations for lawful and charitable objectives.
d. To undertake all lawful activities incidental or conducive to the attainment of the above aims and objects.

6. APPLICATION OF INCOME
All income, earnings, and movable or immovable properties of the Society shall be applied solely towards the promotion of its aims and objects.
No portion thereof shall be paid or transferred directly or indirectly by way of dividend, bonus, profit, or otherwise to any present or past member of the Society.
No member shall have any personal claim over the assets of the Society by virtue of membership.

7. GOVERNING BODY
The affairs of the Society shall be managed by a Governing Body constituted in accordance with the Rules and Regulations of the Society and as required under Section 2 of the Societies Registration Act, 1860.

8. MEMBERSHIP
The Society shall consist of individuals who subscribe to the aims and objects of the Society and are admitted as members in accordance with the Rules and Regulations.

9. DISSOLUTION
In the event of dissolution of the Society, the assets of the Society shall be transferred to another society or organisation having similar charitable objects, in accordance with Sections 13 and 14 of the Societies Registration Act, 1860.
No member shall derive any personal benefit from such dissolution.

10. DECLARATION
We, the undersigned persons, are desirous of forming a Society under the Societies Registration Act, 1860, and hereby subscribe our names to this Memorandum of Association.

RULES AND REGULATIONS
OF THE SOCIETY
“PARENT RIGHTS EDUCATION ASSOCIATION”
(Registered under the Societies Registration Act, 1860, as applicable to the NCT of Delhi)
	•	NAME
The name of the Society shall be “PARENT RIGHTS EDUCATION ASSOCIATION” (hereinafter referred to as “the Society”).
	•	DEFINITIONS
Unless the context otherwise requires:
(i) “Act” means the Societies Registration Act, 1860, as applicable to the National Capital Territory of Delhi.
(ii) “Society” means Parent Rights Education Association.
(iii) “Governing Body” means the body entrusted with the management, control, and administration of the Society.
(iv) “General Body” means the body consisting of all members of the Society.
(v) “Rules” means these Rules and Regulations and includes any amendment made thereto in accordance with law.
	•	MEMBERSHIP
(i) Membership of the Society shall be open to any person of sound mind, good conduct, and repute who subscribes to the aims and objects of the Society.
(ii) Membership shall be granted without discrimination on the grounds of religion, caste, creed, gender, place of birth, domicile, or social status.
(iii) Every applicant for membership shall submit an application in such form and manner as may be prescribed by the Governing Body.
(iv) Admission of a member shall be subject to approval of the Governing Body.
(v) Where membership is refused, the reasons for such refusal shall be recorded in writing and communicated to the applicant.
	•	CATEGORIES OF MEMBERS
The Society may have such categories of members as may be determined by the Governing Body from time to time, including:
(i) Ordinary Members;
(ii) Life Members;
(iii) Honorary Members, who shall not have voting rights unless expressly resolved by the General Body.
	•	SUBSCRIPTION AND FEES
(i) Admission Fee: Rs. ______
(ii) Annual Subscription: Rs. ______
(iii) Life Membership Fee: Rs. ______
(iv) The Governing Body shall have the power to revise, enhance, reduce, or restructure the fee and subscription amounts from time to time, subject to ratification by the General Body where required.
	•	TERMINATION OF MEMBERSHIP
Membership shall cease upon the occurrence of any of the following:
(i) Death or insanity of the member;
(ii) Submission of a written resignation addressed to the Secretary;
(iii) Non-payment of subscription for a continuous period exceeding two years, despite notice;
(iv) Failure to attend three consecutive General Body meetings without sufficient cause;
(v) Expulsion for conduct prejudicial to the aims, objects, discipline, integrity, or interests of the Society.
Provided that no member shall be expelled unless he or she has been given a reasonable opportunity of being heard and the decision is supported by reasons recorded in writing.
	•	APPEAL 
Any person aggrieved by refusal, termination, or expulsion from membership may prefer an appeal before the Governing Body within such time as may be prescribed. The decision of the Governing Body shall be final and binding.
	•	RE-ADMISSION
A terminated or expelled member may be re-admitted upon payment of outstanding dues, if any, and subject to approval of the Governing Body, whose decision shall be final.
	•	RIGHTS AND PRIVILEGES OF MEMBERS
Every member of the Society shall have the following rights and privileges, subject to the provisions of the Act, the Memorandum of Association, and these Rules and Regulations:
(i) To participate in the meetings of the General Body of the Society and to take part in deliberations thereat.
(ii) To exercise the right to vote in favour of or against the election of any office bearer or member of the Governing Body, in accordance with these Rules.
(iii) To propose or second the name of any eligible member for election to the Governing Body, subject to the procedure prescribed by the Society.
(iv) To propose or second any resolution in the General Body, with the prior permission of the President or the person presiding over the meeting.
(v) To vote in favour of or against any resolution placed before the General Body, according to his or her own judgment and conscience, subject to these Rules.
(vi) To contest election for any post of the Governing Body, provided that his or her candidature is duly proposed by one member and seconded by another member of the Society.
(vii) To inspect the books of accounts, records, registers, and documents of the Society, with prior approval of the General Body or in such manner as may be prescribed by the Governing Body.
(viii) To raise objections or seek explanation in respect of any alleged irregularity in the accounts, administration, or records of the Society and to refer the same to the President or the Governing Body for consideration.
 
	•	DUTIES AND OBLIGATIONS OF MEMBERS
Every member of the Society shall be bound by the following duties and obligations:
(i) To furnish true, complete, and necessary information to the Society in respect of any matter which is required for the proper functioning, administration, or compliance of the Society.
(ii) To abide by the aims and objects of the Society and to comply with the Memorandum of Association and these Rules and Regulations at all times.
(iii) Not to engage in any act, conduct, or activity which is prejudicial to, or inconsistent with, the objects, discipline, reputation, or lawful functioning of the Society.
(iv) To maintain the dignity, unity, and collective character of the Society in all dealings with members, authorities, and third parties.
(v) To refrain from misusing the name, platform, or identity of the Society for personal, political, or unlawful purposes.

	•	GENERAL BODY
(i) The General Body shall consist of all members of the Society.
(ii) An Annual General Meeting shall be held at least once in every calendar year.
(iii) Special or emergency general meetings may be convened upon requisition made in writing by not less than one-third of the total members.
(iv) Notice period shall be:
(a) Annual General Meeting – twenty-one days;
(b) Special or Emergency Meeting – seven days.
(v) Quorum shall be two-thirds of the total members present at the meeting.
	•	POWERS AND DUTIES OF THE GENERAL BODY
The General Body, being the supreme authority of the Society, shall exercise the following powers and perform the following duties, subject to the provisions of the Act, the Memorandum of Association, and these Rules and Regulations:
(i) To elect the members of the Governing Body in accordance with the procedure prescribed under these Rules, by a majority of votes of members present and voting at a duly convened General Body meeting.
(ii) To approve, review, and oversee the overall policies, functioning, and activities of the Society, and to act as the supervisory and controlling authority over the Governing Body.
(iii) To consider, approve, or reject decisions, actions, or appointments made by the Governing Body, where such approval is required under these Rules or where the General Body deems such review necessary in the interest of the Society.
(iv) To approve or disapprove any appointment made by the Governing Body to fill a vacancy arising mid-term in the Governing Body, due to resignation, death, disqualification, or otherwise, at the next General Body meeting.
(v) To take all decisions in its meetings by majority of members present and voting, unless otherwise provided in these Rules or under the Act.
(vi) To examine, scrutinise, and review the financial statements, accounts, audit reports, and utilisation of funds of the Society, and to seek explanations for any irregularity, discrepancy, or deviation.
(vii) To act as a watchdog body to ensure that the Governing Body functions in accordance with the Memorandum of Association, these Rules and Regulations, resolutions of the General Body, and applicable laws.
(viii) To exercise all such powers as are vested in the Society under the Memorandum of Association, these Rules and Regulations, and the Societies Registration Act, 1860, insofar as such powers are not expressly delegated to the Governing Body.

	•	Quorum, Notice and Periodicity of Meetings
(i) The General Body of the Society shall meet at least once in every calendar year as an Annual General Meeting. The quorum for such meeting shall be deemed to be complete only when more than two-thirds of the total members of the Society are present. Notice of the Annual General Meeting shall be issued to all members at least fifteen days prior to the date fixed for the meeting.
(ii) In the event that the requisite quorum is not present at the scheduled Annual General Meeting, the meeting shall stand adjourned and shall be reconvened after a minimum period of two weeks. Notice of the adjourned meeting shall be issued to all members at least seven days prior to the date fixed for such adjourned meeting.
(iii) The quorum for the first adjourned General Body meeting shall be one-half of the total members on the rolls of the Society as on the date of such meeting. In case the presence of the required quorum is still not available, the meeting shall be adjourned further for a period of one week, and notice of twenty-four (24) hours shall be sufficient to convene such further adjourned meeting.
(iv) The quorum for the second adjourned General Body meeting shall be one-fourth of the total members on the rolls of the Society at the time of such meeting, and the business transacted thereat shall be valid and binding on all members.
(v) An Urgent or Special General Body Meeting may be convened by the President, or in his or her absence by the Vice President, by giving a notice of not less than twenty-four hours, provided that the quorum for such urgent meeting shall be not less than two-thirds of the total strength of the General Body of the Society.
	•	COMPOSITION OF GOVERNING BODY
(i)The management, control, and administration of the Society shall vest in the Governing Body.
(ii) The Governing Body shall consist of the following office bearers:
(a) President – One
(b) Vice President – One
(c) Secretary – One
(d) Joint Secretary – One
(e) Treasurer – One
(f) Executive Members –  2 to  9 
(iii) Minimum strength of the Governing Body shall be 7 .
(iv) Maximum strength of the Governing Body shall be 11 .
	•	TENURE, ELECTION, MEETINGS AND QUORUM OF THE GOVERNING BODY
(i) Tenure of Governing Body
 The tenure of the Governing Body shall be three (3) years from the date of its election.
(ii) Extension of Tenure
 In the event that elections cannot be held due to unavoidable or exceptional circumstances, the tenure of the existing Governing Body may be extended:
 a) for a first extension of a period not exceeding one (1) year; and
 b) for a second and final extension of a further period not exceeding one (1) year.
 No further extension shall be permissible, and elections shall mandatorily be conducted within the extended period.
(iii) Election Process
 Elections to the Governing Body shall be conducted during the Annual General Meeting of the Society, either by secret ballot or by show of hands, as resolved by the General Body.
(iv) Meetings of the Governing Body
 The Governing Body shall meet at least once every three months.
(v) Notice of Meetings
 A minimum seven (7) days’ prior notice shall be given to all members of the Governing Body for every meeting.
(vi) Quorum for Governing Body Meetings
 The quorum for a meeting of the Governing Body shall be two-thirds (2/3rd) of its total strength, including office bearers and executive members.
(vii) Postponement Due to Lack of Quorum
 (a) If quorum is not present, the meeting shall be postponed for at least two weeks, and a fresh notice of seven (7) days shall be issued.
 (b) The quorum for the postponed meeting shall be one-half (1/2) of the total strength of the Governing Body.
 (c) If quorum is still not present, the meeting shall be postponed further for one week, and a twenty-four (24) hours’ notice shall be sufficient.
 (d) The quorum for such second postponed meeting shall be one-fourth (1/4th) of the total strength of the Governing Body.
(viii) Decision-Making
 All decisions shall be taken by a majority of members present and voting.
 In case of equality of votes, the President shall have a casting vote.
	•	POWERS AND DUTIES OF OFFICE BEARERS
16.1 General Powers
The Governing Body shall be the principal managing authority of the Society and shall exercise powers and perform duties to ensure smooth functioning and promotion of the aims and objects of the Society. Its powers and duties shall include but not be limited to the following:
(i) To manage, supervise, and look after all the day-to-day activities of the Society in furtherance of its aims and objects.
(ii) To execute all resolutions passed by the General Body and ensure their proper implementation.
(iii) To take decisions in its meetings by majority and, if required, constitute one or more sub-committees or assign individual members to execute specific tasks.
(iv) To prepare and approve plans, projects, programmes, and activities of the Society in accordance with its objectives.
(v) To invest funds of the Society not immediately required, in a manner authorised by the Governing Body, while complying with applicable laws and regulations.
(vi) To arrange for and receive loans, advances, or financial support from banks, financial institutions, legal entities, or individuals on reasonable terms, with the Governing Body collectively responsible for repayment.
(vii) To accept donations, grants, gifts, endowments, subscriptions, and contributions for the purposes of the Society.
(viii) To collect funds, whether in cash or kind, and utilise the same, including the income generated therefrom, solely for the objectives of the Society.
(ix) To receive, hold, and manage money, securities, instruments, or any other movable property on behalf of the Society.
(x) To enter into contracts, agreements, or memoranda on behalf of the Society, consistent with its aims and legal requirements.
(xi) To send representatives to exhibitions, competitions, training programmes, or workshops, within or outside India, for capacity-building or promotion of the Society’s objectives.
(xii) To initiate legal proceedings or defend actions in courts, tribunals, or authorities, as may be necessary or proper for safeguarding the Society’s interests and funds, and to ensure enforcement of its objectives.
(xiii) To maintain proper records of all activities, resolutions, transactions, and minutes of the Governing Body meetings during its tenure.
(xiv) To ensure that all income and expenditure accounts of the Society are correctly maintained; the Governing Body shall collectively be responsible for any irregularities.
(xv) To manage and ensure an audit of the Society’s accounts at least once every financial year.
(xvi) To fill temporarily any vacancy arising in the Governing Body, subject to the approval or ratification of the General Body at its next meeting.
(xvii) To convene regular meetings of the Governing Body at least once every three months, with a notice of not less than seven days, and to call emergency meetings on a notice of twenty-four hours if necessary.
(xviii) To appoint Patrons of the Society and define their roles and contributions, if deemed beneficial for the Society.
(xix) To perform any other lawful acts or exercise any powers necessary for the proper management of the Society and for furtherance of its objectives, consistent with the Memorandum of Association and Rules & Regulations.

16.2 President
The President shall be the head and principal executive authority of the Society and shall exercise general superintendence over its affairs.
The President shall have the following powers and duties:
(i) To preside over all meetings of the General Body and the Governing Body.
(ii) To determine, in consultation with the Governing Body, the agenda, venue, date, and time of meetings.
(iii) To give general directions for the management and administration of the Society.
(iv) To represent the Society before courts, tribunals, government authorities, and other institutions, unless such authority is delegated.
 (v) To sanction necessary expenditure, both revenue and capital, for furtherance of the aims and objects of the Society, subject to approval norms prescribed by the Governing Body.
 (vi) To review day-to-day activities and monitor utilisation of funds of the Society.
 (vii) To sign correspondence, documents, and papers on behalf of the Society.
 (viii) To accept or reject applications for membership on the recommendation of the Secretary.
 (ix) To postpone meetings where a quorum is not present.
 (x) To ensure that the accounts of the Society are audited by a qualified auditor appointed by the Governing Body.
 (xi) To exercise a casting vote in case of equality of votes on any matter, except elections.
 (xii) In case of vacancy of any office bearer or executive member during the term, to temporarily discharge or assign such functions until ratification by the governing body or general body, as applicable.
16.3 Vice President
The Vice President shall be an active constitutional functionary and shall assist in governance, compliance, and internal oversight of the Society.
The Vice President shall have the following powers and duties:
(i) To act as President during the absence, incapacity, or recusal of the President, except for sanctioning expenditure unless specifically authorised.
(ii) To preside over meetings when authorised by the President or the Governing Body.
(iii) To oversee compliance with the Memorandum of Association, Rules and Regulations, and applicable statutory requirements.
(iv) To review governance practices and place observations and recommendations before the Governing Body.
(v) To facilitate internal grievance redressal and submit reasoned recommendations for resolution.
(vi) To perform such other duties as may be assigned by these Rules or by resolutions of the Governing Body.
16.4 Secretary
The Secretary shall be the chief administrative and executive officer of the Society, responsible for its day-to-day functioning.
The Secretary shall have the following powers and duties:
(i) To conduct correspondence and represent the Society before governmental and non-governmental authorities, subject to directions of the President.
(ii) To convene meetings of the General Body and Governing Body with the consent of the President.
(iii) To conduct meetings in accordance with the agenda and maintain proper decorum.
(iv) To maintain membership registers, minutes books, records, and statutory filings of the Society.
(v) To implement resolutions passed by the Governing Body and General Body.
(vi) To supervise administrative functioning and activities of the Society.
(vii) To recommend acceptance or rejection of membership applications.
(viii) To maintain detailed records of activities, representations, and proceedings of the Society.
(ix) To incur expenditure up to Rs. 10,000/- at a time, subject to ratification in the next Governing Body meeting.
(x) To incur expenditure as per project budgets duly approved by the Governing Body.
16.5 Joint Secretary
The Joint Secretary shall be a substantive support authority and shall ensure continuity, coordination, and compliance.
The Joint Secretary shall have the following powers and duties:
(i) To assist the Secretary in discharge of official and administrative duties.
(ii) To maintain membership records, documentation, and institutional files.
(iii) To coordinate between members, committees, advocates, and authorities.
(iv) To monitor implementation of resolutions, representations, and legal or administrative proceedings.
(v) To sign routine correspondence when authorised.
(vi) To discharge the duties of the Secretary in his absence, subject to supervision of the President.
(vii) To perform such other duties as may be assigned by the Secretary or the Governing Body.
16.6 Treasurer
The Treasurer shall be the custodian of the finances of the Society.
The Treasurer shall have the following powers and duties:
(i) To receive all funds, donations, grants, and contributions of the Society.
(ii) To maintain true and correct accounts of receipts and expenditure.
(iii) To deposit all funds in scheduled banks or post offices as approved by the Governing Body.
(iv) To withdraw and disburse funds duly sanctioned by the President or Secretary.
(v) To present account books and financial statements at every Governing Body and General Body meeting.
(vi) To keep cash in hand not exceeding Rs. 20,000/- for day-to-day expenses.
(vii) To safeguard all financial records of the Society.
16.7 Executive Members
Executive Members shall actively participate in governance and implementation.
(i) Executive Members shall attend meetings of the Governing Body and General Body.
(ii) They shall assist in execution of activities, programmes, and objectives of the Society.
 (iii) They may be assigned specific responsibilities by the Governing Body or Office Bearers.
16.8 Authority Alignment and Safeguarding Clause
The Vice President and Joint Secretary shall exercise such powers and perform such duties as may be assigned by these Rules or by resolutions of the Governing Body and shall function in aid of, and not in derogation of, the authority of the President and Secretary.
	•	REGULATION OF LEGAL PROCEEDINGS AND COLLECTIVE ACTION
17.1 Restriction on Unauthorised Petitions for Common Cause
No member shall, in his or her individual capacity, initiate, file, sponsor, pursue, support, or project any writ petition, public interest litigation, complaint, representation, or legal proceeding before any court, tribunal, or authority on any cause or issue which is being pursued, proposed to be pursued, or which falls within the objects of the Society, without prior written authorisation of the Governing Body.
17.2 Individual Remedies Not to Prejudice Collective Action
Nothing contained herein shall prevent a member from seeking individual legal remedies for redressal of personal grievances, provided that such action does not restrain, prejudice, obstruct, or undermine collective or institutional action undertaken by the Society.
17.3 Primacy of the Society in Collective Causes
In matters involving issues of common interest, collective rights, statutory compliance, or public importance falling within the objects of the Society, the Society alone shall be the authorised representative body and any legal action shall be initiated only in the name of the Society pursuant to a resolution of the Governing Body.
17.4 Violation and Disciplinary Action
Any member acting in violation of Clauses 15.1 to 15.3 shall be deemed to have acted against the discipline and interests of the Society and shall be liable to disciplinary action including warning, suspension, or termination of membership, after being afforded a reasonable opportunity of hearing.
17.5 Mandatory Disclosure of Legal Proceedings
Every member shall disclose to the Society any legal proceeding initiated or proposed to be initiated by him or her which relates to, overlaps with, or impacts the objects, causes, or issues concerning the Society.
17.6 Unauthorized Representation
No member shall claim, project, or represent himself or herself as representing the Society before any forum unless expressly authorised in writing by the Governing Body, and any such unauthorised act shall constitute misconduct.
	•	 BANK ACCOUNT
The Society shall maintain its bank account in a nationalised or scheduled bank and the same shall be operated jointly by the President and Secretary or by the Secretary and Treasurer, as decided by the Governing Body.
	•	SOURCE OF INCOME
The funds of the Society shall consist of membership fees, subscriptions, donations, grants, and aid received from lawful sources.
	•	APPLICATION OF FUNDS
All funds of the Society shall be applied solely towards promotion of its aims and objects and no part thereof shall be distributed by way of profit or personal benefit.
	•	AUDIT
The accounts of the Society shall be audited annually by a qualified auditor appointed by the General Body.
	•	FINANCIAL YEAR
The financial year of the Society shall commence on the first day of April and end on the thirty-first day of March.
	•	LEGAL PROCEEDINGS
The Society may sue or be sued in the name of the President or the Secretary in accordance with Section 6 of the Act.
	•	AMENDMENT
Any amendment to the Memorandum of Association or these Rules and Regulations shall be made in accordance with Sections 12 and 12-A of the Act.
	•	ANNUAL FILING
An annual list of the names, addresses, and occupations of the office bearers and members of the Governing Body shall be filed with the Registrar as required under Section 4 of the Act.
	•	DISSOLUTION
The Society may be dissolved in accordance with Sections 13 and 14 of the Act and upon dissolution, the surplus assets shall be transferred to another society having similar charitable objects.
	•	APPLICABILITY OF LAW
All provisions of the Societies Registration Act, 1860, as applicable to the National Capital Territory of Delhi, shall apply to the Society.
